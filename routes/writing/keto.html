Killing Post-Prandial Reactive Hyperinsulinemia(Carb Addiction)

I was not a health person. But I mostly quit carbs without counting calories and it totally changed my life.

The following things happened after just 6 months on this:

  • Lost 20 pounds
  • Alleviated/got a handle on some lame mood fluctuations
  • Got my body to where I like showing it off
  • Saved money by changing my philosophy on food

I did it on a very tight budget for a year and a half while working from home.

I had time to cook my meals. My weight fluctuated throughout college. (120-145). I was never very overweight but I was disconnected from my health and felt out of control of what I was eating!

And TBT, I'm still one of those people that, if faced with a loaf of italian bread or a bag of Hershey kisses, could clean house. Yep, to the face. All 15 servings. I love carbs/sugar and carbs/sugar love my belly. A lot of people have this issue and I wanted to share what I do because I've had a lot of success with it and want to spread the self-love I've found through changing my unhealthy carb habits.

I loosely follow the ketogenic diet. I'd call it my own variatian but it sure is Low Carb, High Fat. I take a cheat day once a week during which (lately) I eat three Haagen Daaz Salted ice-cream bars in one sitting.

Six days a week, I eat mostly these things and it's made me a lot happier.
